# **Lithilian**.

---

## Regions

### 🌲 Verdant Crown
A vast, ancient forestland where civilization survives by agreement rather than conquest. The trees here feel older than borders, and the Wild presses close even near settled roads. The Crown is not “untamed” so much as *unfathomably patient*.

### 🌫️ Veilmoor
Cold moors and fog-drowned lowlands where distance lies and sound carries wrong. Travelers swear roads shift by a few degrees on mist nights, and strange lights drift along the waterline. Veilmoor is a threshold region: safe enough to cross, eerie enough to remember.

### 🌾 Lanternfields
The breadbasket of the continent and the heart of its stability. Wide plains, farms, trade towns, and the densest Lantern coverage make it feel safer than anywhere else. But safety here is a built thing, maintained by work, law, and the constant fear of what happens if the lights fail.

### 🏔️ Frostback Mountains
A northern wall of stone and ice that divides the world into “before” and “after.” The Frostbacks are riddled with deep halls and older secrets, and the passes are infamous choke points where caravans and armies alike become vulnerable. The mountains do not merely block travel; they shape history.

### 🔥🏜️ Cinderstep Expanse
A harsh sweep of red rock, ashland, and wind-carved canyons where water and shade are currency. Settlements here are fortresses by necessity, and roads are measured by endurance rather than miles. The Cindersteps breed hard people and harder stories.

### 🌊 Ember Gulf
Warm southern waters and coastal trade routes that tie distant regions together. The Gulf’s ports thrive on commerce, ships, and politics, and its shores collect wealth the way a storm collects driftwood. It’s a place of opportunity with sharp edges, where every deal has a shadow.

### 🌑 Black Reach
A closed-basin swamp where water gathers and never truly leaves. Channels shift, ground sinks, and old things stay buried only because the mud is kind enough not to spit them back out. Lantern light doesn’t die here… it simply feels smaller.

### 🌩️ Stormbreak Coast
A wind-lashed coast of cliffs, rough seas, and stubborn fort-towns. Wrecks and ruins dot the shoreline, and storms arrive like sieges. It’s a hard place to live, but the people who do become the kind who don’t blink first.

### ❄️🌲 Frostpine Wilds
Snowy boreal forests that spill up into the northern Frostback foothills. The pines grow tall and tight, swallowing sound beneath heavy snow, and the cold teaches respect quickly. It’s a land of long tracks, quiet predators, and travelers who learn to love silence.

### 🌲 Brightwood
The lighter, more traveled forest surrounding Rosegate, where paths are maintained and the canopy feels kinder. It’s still the Crown’s edge, still old, but it’s a *negotiated wilderness*—a place where people can breathe before the deeper woods begin to watch.

## Waters

### 🌊 Mistral Deep
The western ocean bordering Lithilian’s far coast, known for steady winds, long swells, and fog-prone horizons.

### 🌊 Stormglass Sea
The eastern ocean, where sharp winds and restless waters grind the shoreline and turn ship travel into a wager.

---

## Islands

### 🐉 Skybreak Isle
A northeastern island known for one simple truth: **dragons are real here**. Skybreak is not defined by storms or mystery, but by territory—claimed air, claimed stone, claimed silence. Those who approach do so with reverence, fear, or a plan so clever it usually fails.

## Settlements

### 🌲 Rosegate (Capital)
A human-led capital set at the civilized edge of the Verdant Crown, where politics meets ancient green. Rosegate is multicultural by necessity—trade, diplomacy, and Lanternkeeping all converge here. It’s the kind of place where every road carries news, and every decision has consequences beyond the city walls.

### 🌫️ Highgarden (Elven City)
An elven city of Veilmoor, tied to Mistveil Lake and the mistlands’ slow, quiet power. Highgarden is equal parts sanctuary and seat of influence, where patience is treated as strength. Outsiders often leave with the uneasy feeling they were understood too well.

### 🏔️ Stonehield (West Dwarven Minehold)
A fortress-mine carved into the western Frostbacks, built to endure both winter and war. Stonehield’s strength isn’t just in its walls but in its discipline—everything here feels measured, tested, and reinforced. If a pass must hold, it holds from Stonehield’s side.

### 🏔️ Frostgate (East Dwarven Minehold)
A hardened minehold in the eastern Frostbacks, positioned near the colder approaches and harsher winds. Frostgate is known as a threshold: beyond it the world becomes sharper, colder, and less forgiving. Dwarves here speak in practical terms—ore, stone, duty, and the cost of keeping roads open.

### 🌾 Waystone (Lanternfields Town)
A crossroads town where routes meet and travelers regroup, named as much for guidance as for geography. Waystone is the practical heart of movement through the Lanternfields—caravans, messengers, and adventurers all pass through its inns and counting-houses. If you want to hear what’s happening anywhere, you listen in Waystone.

### 🌾 Riverbend (Lanternfields Town)
A settlement shaped by water, trade, and steady labor. Riverbend feels calmer than most places in Lithilian, with fields, mills, and reliable routes nearby. It’s also where people learn that “safe” doesn’t mean “simple.”

### 🌊 Farharbor (Gulfward Town)
A gulf-facing harbor town that bridges inland trade with coastal wealth. Farharbor is busy, practical, and always watching the horizon—storms, ships, and rumors arrive the same way. It’s a place where opportunity smells like salt and coin.

### 🌲 Lanternpost (Thornvale Outpost)
A frontier outpost at Thornvale’s edge, where the forest begins to feel like a living boundary. Lanternpost exists to keep the road honest—repairs, warnings, escorts, and the kind of quiet heroism that never makes songs. Travelers sleep better here, even if they don’t admit it.

### 🌲 Deepwatch (Thornvale Outpost)
A deeper Thornvale watchpoint where the wilderness has more votes than the road. Deepwatch is smaller, tougher, and more isolated than Lanternpost, with a culture shaped by vigilance. The people here don’t fear the dark as much as they distrust what’s *quiet* within it.

### 🌑 Marshgate (Black Reach Outpost)
An outpost on the rim of the Black Reach basin, built to endure mud, fog, and bad decisions. Marshgate is part checkpoint, part lifeline, part warning sign. Everyone who goes deeper starts here—and most of the sensible ones come back.

### 🔥🏜️ Sunhold (Cinderstep Fort City)
A fortified city holding the northern approaches to the Cinderstep Expanse. Sunhold is stone, heat, and hard rules—built to keep people alive as much as to keep enemies out. Its walls stand like a statement: *the desert doesn’t get everything*.

### 🔥🏜️ Cinderpost (Cinderstep Outpost)
A remote outpost deeper in the Cindersteps, more survival station than town. Cinderpost exists because someone has to, a dot of endurance on a hostile route. It’s the kind of place where water is counted twice and strangers are weighed carefully.

## Roads and Passes

### 🛣️ Rose Road
The main connection between Rosegate and Highgarden, skirting the edge of the Crown and threading into the mistlands. It’s a road of diplomats, couriers, and trade caravans—busy enough to feel safe, old enough to have ghosts in its ditches.

### 🛣️ Fields Road
The working artery between Highgarden and Waystone, used by merchants and farmers as much as adventurers. It’s wide where it can be, muddy where it must be, and always full of small stories that become big ones later.

### 🛣️ Thorn Road
The route into Thornvale, running from Waystone to Lanternpost and onward to Deepwatch. It’s a road that teaches travelers the difference between “forest” and “wilderness.” Lantern light helps, but it does not make the trees friendly.

### 🛣️ Red Road
The road from Waystone into Sunhold and deeper toward Cinderpost, marked by dust, stone, and sun-baked distance. It’s a route where logistics matter and mistakes have teeth. Those who travel it often come back changed.

### 🛣️ Bogway
A treacherous approach from Waystone toward Marshgate, edging into the Black Reach basin. The ground softens, the air thickens, and direction becomes a negotiation. Bogway is less a road than a promise someone keeps trying to make true.

### 🏔️ Stonepass
A primary western pass through the Frostbacks leading toward Stonehield. Heavily watched, heavily used, and often the first to become political during conflict.

### 🏔️ Frostpass
A primary eastern pass leading toward Frostgate, colder and sharper than Stonepass. When it’s open, it matters. When it’s closed, people start panicking.

### 🏔️ Northpass
A harsh, rarely used high pass between Stonehield and Frostgate. The kind of route spoken of in the same breath as cautionary tales—possible, but never casual.
