
# Items: Types & Unique Rarity

All items belong to one **Item Type**. Some items—those that are truly one-of-a-kind—also carry a **Unique Rarity**.

---

## Item Type

### Mundane
A mundane item is non-magical. It may be well-made, beautifully crafted, or suspiciously heavy, but it contains no magical power.

### Magic
A magic item possesses at least one magical property (a blessing, rune, enchantment, or other supernatural effect). Magic items may be mass-produced, replicated, or otherwise "not special" in the grand sense—only magical.

### Unique
A unique item is **named** and **singular**. It carries a distinct identity that cannot be replaced by simply crafting another copy. Unique items often include bespoke effects, unusual rules, or properties that behave in ways ordinary magic does not. If it has a name people remember, it's probably Unique.

---

## Unique Rarity
Unique rarity is used **only** for items with the **Unique** item type.

### Notable
Uncommon named items with a clear identity. People talk about them. Sometimes they even spell the name right.

### Renowned
Rare and respected. Known beyond a single town, and often tied to an event, tradition, or notable bearer.

### Legendary
Famous enough to become story fuel. Legends disagree on the details, but everyone agrees the item matters.

### Mythic
The highest rarity. A Mythic Unique is inseparable from its **lore**—origin, consequence, and reputation. Mythic items don't merely *exist* in the world; they leave marks on it.  

**Rule:** Every Mythic Unique includes lore.

### Amulets

#### **Ember's Last Breath** — *Mythic*
**Cost:** 3,500g  
**Materials:** Cinderglass pendant with dragonbone chain  
**Requires Attunement**

**Effects:**
- The lantern you carry never runs out of fuel and cannot be extinguished by wind or water
- Once per long rest, you may consume the flame from your lantern to unleash an explosion in a 15-foot radius. All creatures in the area take 6d6 fire damage (DEX save DC 15 for half). Allies in the area instead heal 3d6 HP
- After using this ability, your lantern is dark for 1 hour before reigniting

**Lore:**  
Forged in the final moments before the First Forge collapsed, this amulet holds a fragment of that eternal flame. It was worn by Ember Greystone, a Lanternkeeper who stayed behind to guide the last survivors out. The flame never died, but Ember did. Some say her spirit still wanders the ruins, looking for one more soul to save.

#### **Lanternkeeper's Promise** — *Mythic*
**Cost:** Cannot be bought (earned through service to the Lantern Network)  
**Materials:** Silver compass rose with an eternal flame at its center  
**Requires Attunement**

**Effects:**
- You always know the direction and approximate distance to the nearest Safe House in the Lantern Network
- While within the light of a lantern, you gain +1 to all saving throws
- Once per day, you can send a message of up to 50 words through any flame to another Lanternkeeper or Safe House

**Lore:**  
Given only to those who have proven themselves to the Lantern Network—not through gold or status, but through sacrifice. Each Promise is inscribed with the bearer's name and the date they earned it. When a Lanternkeeper falls, their Promise returns to the Network, waiting for the next worthy soul. The flame at its center is said to burn brighter the closer you are to home.

#### **Veil of the Forgotten** — *Mythic*
**Cost:** 2,800g (cursed, but some pay anyway)  
**Materials:** Black obsidian disc wrapped in grave ash silk  
**Requires Attunement**

**Effects:**
- You cast no shadow in any light
- Twice per long rest, you can become invisible for up to 10 minutes (concentration). The effect ends if you attack or cast a spell
- While invisible, the Shadow can sense your presence. Make a WIS saving throw (DC 13). On a failure, you draw unwanted attention

**Lore:**  
Mira Blackhand was the finest assassin in three kingdoms. She never failed. She never left a trace. She never cast a shadow. Until the day she vanished entirely—mid-contract, mid-step, mid-breath. They found the Veil in the alley where she was last seen, still warm. No one knows if she finally slipped into the Shadow for good, or if the Shadow finally claimed her. Either way, the Veil remembers.

#### **Crown of Distant Stars** — *Mythic*
**Cost:** 4,500g (rare, usually gifted or inherited)  
**Materials:** Platinum band set with seven star sapphires  
**Requires Attunement**

**Effects:**
- Gain darkvision out to 120 feet. If you already have darkvision, extend it by 60 feet
- Once per long rest, you can ask the stars a single yes/no question about the future. The DM answers truthfully, but the stars speak in symbols—interpretation is yours
- Once per long rest, you can call down starlight in a 20-foot radius. Choose: deal 4d10 radiant damage to all creatures in the area (CON save DC 15 for half), or heal all allies in the area for 4d10 HP

**Lore:**  
A gift from the High Elves of the Starwood to the human astronomer Lyra Stargazer, who mapped the night sky so precisely that sailors no longer feared the open ocean. She wore it every night, recording the positions of stars that hadn't moved in a thousand years. On her final night, she asked the Crown one last question. No one knows what she asked. She smiled, set down her pen, and walked into the stars. The Crown was found on her desk, still glowing faintly.

#### **The Hollow Heart** — *Mythic*
**Cost:** 1,800g (those who need it usually can't afford it; those who can afford it usually shouldn't have it)  
**Materials:** Voidsteel shaped like a heart, hollow in the center  
**Requires Attunement**

**Effects:**
- You are immune to poison, disease, and exhaustion
- You no longer need to eat, drink, breathe, or sleep (though you still need to rest to regain abilities)
- Undead creatures treat you as one of their own unless you attack them first
- **Warning:** While attuned, you cannot regain hit points naturally during a long rest. You must use healing magic or consumables

**Lore:**  
No one remembers who made the Hollow Heart, but everyone remembers who wore it last: a plague doctor named Vale, who walked into quarantined zones without fear, tended to the dying without rest, and emerged untouched by rot or ruin. When the plague finally burned itself out, Vale simply... left. No grave. No goodbye. Some say they still wander the roads between towns, appearing wherever sickness strikes. Others say Vale became something else—not quite living, not quite dead. The Hollow Heart is a bridge between two worlds. Be careful which side you stand on.
