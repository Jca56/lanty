# ⚔️🛡️ Equipment System

> **"A warrior is only as good as their blade, and a blade is only as good as its master."**

---

## Philosophy

Every weapon should feel unique. Every armor choice should matter. Gear is customizable, situational, and progresses with your character.

---

## 🗡️ Weapon System

### Weapon Rules

**Stat Requirements:**
- All weapons have minimum STR or DEX requirements
- **Doesn't meet requirement:** Deal half damage with the weapon
- **Meets requirement:** Use weapon normally
- **Has Proficiency:** Add proficiency bonus to attack rolls

*Note: Some Tempers (feats) can reduce stat requirements by 2-3.*

## Weapon Materials

Most weapons (85%) are made of **Steel** (standard), but rare materials provide unique properties. Material affects weapon cost and may add special abilities.

### Material Types & Effects

#### 🥉 Bronze (Common, Outdated)
- **Cost:** 50% of steel price
- **Effect:** -1 damage (minimum 1)
- **Lore:** Cheap and outdated, but accessible
- **Availability:** Common in poor regions, early-game equipment

#### ⚔️ Steel (Standard Baseline)
- **Cost:** 100% (base price)
- **Effect:** None (default)
- **Lore:** Standard forged steel
- **Availability:** Common (85% of all weapons)

#### 🛡️ Deepsteel (Rare, Dwarven)
- **Cost:** 5× steel price
- **Effect:** **Armor Piercing** — Ignore 2 AR when attacking
- **Lore:** Mined from the deepest mountains, incredibly dense metal
- **Availability:** Rare (Dwarven forges, ancient ruins)
- **Appearance:** Dark gray, almost black, with subtle blue sheen

#### 🔥 Cinderglass (Rare, Cinderborn)
- **Cost:** 5× steel price
- **Effect:** **Ember Strike** — On critical hit, target takes +1d6 fire damage and ignites (1d6 fire/turn, DC 13 DEX save to extinguish)
- **Special:** Cinderborn wielders can activate Ember Strike 1/short rest without critical
- **Lore:** Forged in volcanic forges, obsidian-like volcanic glass
- **Availability:** Rare (Cinderborn settlements, lava forges)
- **Appearance:** Black glass with orange/red veins, warm to touch

#### 💀 Grave Ash (Rare, Hollowed)
- **Cost:** 5× steel price
- **Effect:** **Life Drain** — On hit, heal for 1d4 HP (once per turn)
- **Lore:** Forged from ash of the dead and blackened steel
- **Availability:** Rare (Hollowed territories, necromancer lairs)
- **Appearance:** Ash-gray metal with black marbling, cold to touch
- **Note:** Hollowed immune to "cursed weapon" social penalties

#### ✨ Mithril (Very Rare, Elven)
- **Cost:** 10× steel price
- **Effect:** **Lightweight Mastery** — Reduce stat requirement by 3 (minimum 6), +1 to attack rolls, weapon counts as one weight class lighter
- **Lore:** Elven-forged silvery-blue metal, impossibly light yet strong
- **Availability:** Very Rare (High Elf cities, ancient elven armories)
- **Appearance:** Silvery-blue sheen, nearly weightless feel
- **Example:** Mithril Greatsword requires STR 12 instead of STR 15

#### 🐉 Dragonbone (Very Rare, Elemental)
- **Cost:** 10× steel price
- **Effect:** **Elemental Affinity** — Choose damage type when crafted (Fire/Cold/Lightning/Acid/Poison), weapon deals +1d4 of that damage type
- **Lore:** Carved from dragon bones, retains elemental power
- **Availability:** Very Rare (Dragon hoards, Dragonkin tribal vaults)
- **Appearance:** Bone-white with colored veins matching element
- **Variants:** Red Dragonbone (+1d4 fire), Blue (+1d4 lightning), etc.

#### 🌿 Ironwood (Uncommon, Primal)
- **Cost:** 3× steel price
- **Effect:** **Nature's Resilience** — Wielder gains +1 to Nature saves, weapon regenerates 1 HP damage per day
- **Special:** Druids can "awaken" for +2 damage vs constructs/undead
- **Lore:** Living wood harder than steel, grown not forged by Sylvari
- **Availability:** Uncommon (Sylvari groves, Druid circles)
- **Appearance:** Dark wood with green grain, slightly warm
- **Restriction:** Only for bludgeoning/piercing weapons (no blades)

## Weapon Quality Tiers

| Quality | To Hit | Damage | Mod Slots | Traits | Enchantment Slots | Special Ability | Example |
|--------|--------|--------|-----------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Common** | +0 | +0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | None | Mass-produced blade |
| **Fine** | +0 | +0 | 1 | 0 | 0 | None | Well-crafted blade |
| **Superior** | +1 | +0 | 2 | 1 | 1 | None* | Exceptional quality |
| **Masterwork** | +2 | +1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | Optional magic | Perfect craftsmanship |
| **Legendary** | +3 | +2 | 3 | 2-3 | 3 | Unique named ability | Hero's weapon |
| **Artifact** | +4 | +3 | 4 | 3-4 | 4+ | Multiple abilities | World-changing relic |

*_Superior and higher tiers can have enchantments added. Masterwork items are often found unenchanted, creating opportunities for customization._

**Important Distinction:**
- **Quality** determines the item's base quality (stats, mod slots, traits)
- **Enchantments** are magical properties added to Superior+ items
- A **Masterwork Longsword** could be purely mundane (perfect steel) or have 0-2 enchantments
- Master blacksmiths create Masterwork items; enchanters add magic
- Finding an unenchanted Masterwork weapon is valuable - you can customize it!

## Enchantments

**Enchantments** are permanent magical properties added to weapons and armor. They cannot be removed or replaced once applied.

### Enchantment Slots

Each item quality tier has a maximum number of enchantments it can hold:

| Quality | Enchantment Slots |
|--------|-------------------|
| Common | 0 (cannot be enchanted) |
| Fine | 0 (cannot be enchanted) |
| Superior | 1 |
| Masterwork | 2 |
| Legendary | 3 |
| Artifact | 4+ |

### Adding Enchantments

- Requires an enchanter (NPC or party member with enchanting skill)
- Costs gold and rare materials (see Crafting & Enchanting document)
- Takes time (1-7 days depending on enchantment power)
- Once applied, enchantments are **permanent** - choose wisely!

### Common Weapon Enchantments

**Elemental Damage (Minor)** - 1 slot  
Add +1d4 elemental damage (fire, cold, lightning, acid, or poison) to all attacks.

**Elemental Damage (Major)** - 1 slot  
Add +1d6 elemental damage. Requires Masterwork or higher.

**Keen Edge** - 1 slot  
Critical hit range increased by 1 (19-20 instead of 20).

**Returning** - 1 slot  
Thrown weapons return to your hand after attacks.

**Vampiric** - 1 slot  
On hit, heal for half the damage dealt (rounded down). Once per turn.

**Bane (Creature Type)** - 1 slot  
+2d6 damage vs specific creature type (undead, fiends, dragons, etc.).

**Defending** - 1 slot  
Can use bonus action to transfer up to +2 attack bonus to AR instead.

**Vorpal** - 2 slots (Legendary only)  
On natural 20, roll again. If you hit, target is decapitated (if it has a head).

**Life Stealing** - 2 slots  
On kill, gain temporary HP equal to creature's level × 5.

### Common Armor Enchantments

**Resistance (Element)** - 1 slot  
Resistance to one damage type (fire, cold, lightning, acid, poison, necrotic, radiant).

**Fortification** - 1 slot  
+1 AR.

**Evasion** - 1 slot  
+1 ER.

**Spell Resistance** - 1 slot  
Advantage on saves vs spells.

**Energy Ward** - 2 slots  
Resistance to two damage types.

**Ethereal** - 2 slots  
Once per day, become ethereal for 1 minute (can pass through walls, immune to physical damage).

## Weapon Properties Explained

**Light:** Can be dual-wielded, weighs less  
**Heavy:** Small creatures have disadvantage on attacks  
**Finesse:** Use DEX instead of STR for attacks  
**Versatile:** Can be used one-handed or two-handed (higher damage)  
**Reach:** Attack range is 10ft instead of 5ft  
**Two-Handed:** Requires both hands to use  
**Loading:** Can only fire once per turn regardless of attacks  
**Throwable (X/Y):** Normal range X ft, max range Y ft

# Weapon Traits (v1)

Weapon traits define how a weapon applies pressure, exploits openings, and interacts with armor, endurance, and positioning.  
Traits should encourage tactical decisions rather than passive bonuses.

---

## ⚔️ Offensive Traits

### Keen
Improves your chance to score critical hits when you create an opening.

> Rewards precision without pure crit-fishing.

---

### Brutal
Critical hits with this weapon deal significantly increased damage.

> Clean, readable, and expected — but not stacked with other crit traits.

---

### Rending
This weapon is especially effective at breaking through armor, reducing effective AR on certain attacks.

> Armor interaction without invalidating AR.

---

### Precise
Gain bonuses to attack rolls when striking well-defended or heavily armored targets.

> Makes high-AR enemies a puzzle, not a wall.

---

### Heavy Blow
Powerful strikes with this weapon deal increased damage but demand commitment.

> High risk, high reward — very Flame Bound.

---

### Relentless
Repeated successful hits with this weapon become increasingly dangerous.

> Momentum without raw damage stacking.

---

## 🛡️ Defensive & Reactive Traits

### Parrying
You may spend AP to reactively improve your defense against a single attack.

> Explicit AP interaction. Very on-theme.

---

### Guarded
This weapon is difficult to knock aside or disarm.

> Defensive identity without passive stat inflation.

---

### Balanced
This weapon is stable and responsive, improving control while wielded.

> Subtle, reliable, non-explosive.

---

## 🔥 AP & Endurance Interaction Traits

### Swift
Certain attacks with this weapon cost less AP under specific conditions.

> Replaces flat AP reduction abuse.

---

### Committed
This weapon rewards spending additional AP on a single decisive strike.

> Great hook for advanced maneuvers.

---

### Exhausting
This weapon inflicts additional ER pressure on both wielder and target.

> Perfect for brutal, heavy weapons.

---

## 🧭 Reach & Control Traits

### Reach
This weapon can strike enemies at extended range.

> Built-in property, not trait-spammed.

---

### Hooked
On a successful hit, you may spend AP to reposition or disrupt the target.

> Positioning over raw damage.

---

### Sweeping
This weapon excels at controlling space and threatening multiple foes.

> Zone control without AoE spam.

---

## 🎯 Conditions & Wounds

### Bleeding
Critical hits or powerful strikes inflict ongoing damage over time.

> Persistent pressure, not burst nonsense.

---

### Crippling
Strikes can impair movement or positioning when they hit hard.

> Tactical debuff, not save-or-suck.

---

### Disarming
Well-timed attacks can force enemies to lose control of held items.

> Skill expression, not crit lottery.

---

### Igniting
This weapon can set targets ablaze under the right conditions.

> Fire as a state, not just extra dice.

---

## 🧪 Utility & Special Traits

### Quick Draw
This weapon can be readied instantly.

> Low complexity, high QoL.

---

### Throwable
This weapon can be thrown effectively.

> Property, not magic.

---

### Returning
Thrown weapons with this trait return when conditions are met.

> Rare, controlled convenience.

---

### Reservoir
This weapon can hold and deliver a single dose of poison or alchemical agent.

> Cleaner than auto-apply poison.

---

## 🐉 Foe-Specific Traits (Uncommon+)

These traits should be **rare** and **intentional**, never stacked freely.

### Silvered
Effective against creatures vulnerable to silver.

---

### Cold Iron
Bypasses resistances of fey and similar beings.

---

### Sanctified
Deals increased harm to undead and profane entities.

---

### Dragonbane
Exceptionally dangerous to dragons and dragonkin.

---

## ✨ Legendary Traits (Very Rare)

### Vorpal
When you achieve a perfectly executed strike under extreme conditions, devastating results may occur.

> No instant death roulette. Always player-driven.

---

## Trait Design Guidelines

- Avoid stacking multiple crit-only traits
- Prefer AP, AR, ER, or positioning interaction
- Rare traits should **change play**, not inflate numbers
- Legendary traits should feel intentional, not random

Weapons should feel *dangerous because of how they are used*, not because of passive math.

# Weapon Modifications (v1)

Weapons may be fitted with interchangeable modifications based on quality tier.  
Modifications represent physical alterations and can be swapped during a short or long rest.

Modifications should enhance *how* a weapon is used, not simply increase base numbers.

---

## 🖐️ Grip Modifications

### Leather Wrap
Improves control during extended combat, reducing penalties on sustained attacks.

> Reliable, subtle, fatigue-focused.

---

### Spiked Grip
When grappling or locked in close combat, this weapon becomes more dangerous.

> Makes close-quarters fighting scarier without raw damage buffs.

---

### Rubberized Grip
Gain increased resistance against being disarmed or losing control of this weapon.

> Defensive stability, not immunity.

---

### Ergonomic Grip
Once per turn, reduce the AP cost of a weapon action **if no other AP reduction was used**.

> Hard cap prevents abuse. Very Flame Bound.

### Spiked Guard

> Reactive, earned, satisfying.

---

### Basket Hilt
This weapon is exceptionally difficult to disarm, even under pressure.

> Strong, but not absolute.

---

### Hooked Guard
On a successful parry, you may spend AP to disrupt or reposition the attacker.

> Control beats damage.

---

## ⚙️ Blade / Head Modifications

### Serrated Edge
Powerful or critical hits inflict ongoing wounds that worsen if ignored.

> Bleeding as pressure, not free damage.

---

### Weighted Head
This weapon strikes with greater force but demands commitment.

> Damage ↔ mobility tradeoff preserved.

---

### Honed Edge
This weapon is exceptionally sharp, improving effectiveness against exposed or vulnerable targets.

> Precision without crit fishing.

### Tempered Core
The weapon handles extreme stress better, improving performance during prolonged fights.

---

## 🔥 Elemental & Alchemical Mods

### Elemental Channel
The weapon can be primed to release elemental energy on specific attacks.

> Elemental damage becomes intentional, not passive.

---

### Alchemical Groove
This weapon can safely deliver a prepared alchemical agent.

> Replaces auto-poison nonsense cleanly.

---

## 🔩 Pommel & Counterbalance Mods

### Counterweight
Improves balance, making follow-up attacks more reliable after a miss.

> Momentum correction instead of +to-hit spam.

---

### Striking Pommel
Unlocks a close-range pommel strike option.

> Tool, not stun-lock button.

---

### Resonant Pommel
This weapon responds to internal energy, storing or channeling power for specific techniques.

---

## Modification Design Guidelines

- Mods should be **physical** and **visible**
- Mods should encourage **AP choices**
- No mod should fully replace a weapon trait
- Mods are about *expression*, not escalation

Weapons should feel **forged, tuned, and personal** — not enchanted stat sticks.

# 🛡️ Armor

Armor in **Flame Bound** is not a ladder of "better." It's a choice of *how* you survive.

Some travelers survive by never being touched: slipping between blades like smoke in wind. Others survive by letting the blow land, trusting steel and stance to do the talking. The Wild respects both. The Shadow respects neither.

> *Cinder Nutkin, the Lantern-Tail, was once offered a knight's gleaming cuirass before a long road through Lanternless Pass. He tapped the breastplate, smiled, and refused.*  
> *"Steel makes a promise," he said. "Roads break them."*

This chapter is written so mechanics are easy to find at a glance, and flavor is there for those who listen for it.

---

## ⚙️ The Two Ratings: ER and AR

### **Evasion Rating (ER)**
**ER** represents your ability to avoid being hit.
- Your **Hit Target Number** is: **TN = 10 + ER**
- Higher ER means enemies miss more often.

### **Armor Rating (AR)**
**AR** represents your ability to reduce damage after being hit.
- When you take damage from an attack, reduce that damage by your **total AR** (minimum 0).

> ER decides whether the blow lands.  
> AR decides whether the blow matters.

---

## 🧩 Armor Pieces and Slots

Core armor uses **four** pieces:

1. **Chest** *(defines your tier)*
2. **Legs**
3. **Boots**
4. **Helmet** *(optional, but helpful)*

### Gloves (Intentional Omission)
**Gloves are not part of the core armor system.**
- Most gloves are **flavor gear** (warmth, grip, work gloves, ritual wraps).
- Gloves with mechanical effects exist as **Unique items** with explicit rules.

This is intentional: armor is already complex enough without adding "mandatory" filler slots.

---

## 🧱 Armor Tiers

Armor is organized into **four tiers**, each with a clear identity:

| Tier | Identity | What It Excels At |
|---|---|---|
| **Light** | Evasion-first | High ER, minimal AR |
| **Reinforced** | Agile protection | ER + meaningful AR |
| **Braced** | Endurance-first | High AR, low ER |
| **Heavy** | Fortress mode | Massive AR, reduced ER |

### **Tier Rule: Chest Defines Tier (Locked)**
Your **Chest piece defines your armor tier**.  
This keeps armor readable and prevents "tier salad" optimization.

You may still wear mismatched pieces (old repairs, scavenged gear, battlefield necessity), but the **identity** of your defense comes from your core.

## 🎓 Armor Proficiency

Armor proficiency represents trained movement, stance, and fighting posture within that tier.

### Wearing Armor Without Proficiency (Rebalanced)
You may wear armor without proficiency, but it fights *you* as much as the enemy does.

- You suffer **–2 ER** while wearing armor you are not proficient with.
- Armor still provides its **AR** (it still blocks, but you move poorly inside it).

---

## 🧱 Stat Requirements (Armor)

Some armor demands strength and conditioning to wear effectively.

- If you do **not** meet a piece's STR requirement, reduce that piece's **AR by 2** (minimum 0).

**Suggested Requirements (GM may adjust by culture and availability):**
- **Light:** None
- **Reinforced:** None
- **Braced:** STR 10
- **Heavy:** STR 13
- **Bastion Plate:** STR 15

---

## 🕯️ Stealth, Donning, and Sleep

### Stealth
- **Light:** No inherent penalty
- **Reinforced:** No inherent penalty (unless a trait says otherwise)
- **Braced:** Typically imposes Stealth disadvantage unless the armor is **Silent**
- **Heavy:** Typically imposes Stealth disadvantage unless the armor is **Silent**

### Donning and Doffing
- **Light:** Don 1 minute, Doff 1 minute
- **Reinforced:** Don 3 minutes, Doff 1 minute
- **Braced:** Don 5 minutes, Doff 1 minute
- **Heavy:** Don 10 minutes, Doff 5 minutes
- **With help:** Halve the time (an ally must spend the same time assisting)

### Sleeping in Armor
- **Light / Reinforced:** No penalty
- **Braced:** Comfortable only with proficiency (otherwise Long Rest benefits may be reduced at GM discretion)
- **Heavy:** Sleeping in heavy armor is a bad bargain. Use common sense, or a very forgiving bed.

---

## 🏷️ Quality, Mods, and Enchantments (Chest Only)

To keep armor readable and avoid "four separate upgrade trees," **Quality, Mods, and Enchantments apply only to the Chest piece**.

- **Legs, Boots, and Helmets** use their base values and any explicit Unique rules.
- If a non-chest armor piece has mechanics, it is a **Unique item**.

This keeps builds clean while letting the most important piece carry craftsmanship and magic.

> *A master smith can perfect a cuirass to sing with the wearer's breath.*  
> *Boots are still boots. Even if they're excellent boots.*

---

### Armor Quality (Chest Only)

Quality reflects craftsmanship, fit, and finishing. It is not "magic," but great craftsmanship can hold magic better.

| Quality | Chest AR Bonus | Traits on Chest | Enchantment Slots (Chest) | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Common** | +0 | 0 | 0 | Standard issue |
| **Fine** | +0 | 1 | 0 | Well-crafted |
| **Superior** | +1 | 2 | 1 | Exceptional work |
| **Masterwork** | +2 | 2 | 2 | Near-perfect |
| **Legendary** | +3 | 3 + unique ability | 3 | Named legend |
| **Artifact** | +4 | 4 + multiple abilities | 4+ | World-marking relic |

**Rule:** Add the **Chest AR Bonus** to that chest piece's AR.

## 🛡️ Shields (Armor Adjacent)

Shields occupy your **off-hand slot** and provide AR bonuses by blocking and bracing.  
They do not affect your armor tier.

You cannot dual wield, cast spells with somatic components, or use two-handed weapons while wielding a shield.

### Shield Proficiency
- **With proficiency:** You gain the shield's AR normally.
- **Without proficiency:**
  - The shield grants **2 less AR** (minimum 0), and
  - You suffer **–2 ER** while the shield is readied.
  - Cannot attack same turn you use shield bonus.

### Shield Types

| **Standard Shield** | +2 AR | 0 | STR 10 | Full hand commitment, most common shield type |

## 🧥 Capes and Cloaks (Flavor Gear)

Capes and cloaks provide **no direct mechanical bonuses**.

They exist for:
- warmth and weather,
- cultural identity,
- social signaling,
- and silhouette (the world reads you before it hears you).

Common examples:
- **Traveler's Cloak** (patched, road-worn, practical)
- **Ranger's Cloak** (earth-toned, weathered, always present)
- **Soldier's Mantle** (broad cloth worn with pride or rank)
- **Duelist's Half-Cape** (dramatic, confident, loud without speaking)
- **Hooded Cloak** (mysterious, suspicious in towns, beloved by rogues)

Any cloak or cape with mechanical effects is explicitly labeled as a **Unique item**.

## 🏛️ Unique Armor (Where the Cool Stuff Lives)

Core armor is common and readable. **Unique armor** is where legends begin.

Unique armor exists outside standard lists and explicitly lists:
- its tier,
- its piece values,
- and any traits, mods, enchantments, or special rules.

> *Some armor is made to protect you.*  
> *Some armor is made to remind the world who you are.*  
> *And some armor is a warning left behind, so the next traveler turns back before the road grows quiet.*

## 🎯 Power Budget System

Each item has a "power budget" based on quality tier - this helps balance items and create consistent progression:

| Quality | Power Budget | What You Get |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| **Common** | 0 points | Just base stats |
| **Fine** | 2 points | +1 to hit OR +1 damage, 1 mod slot |
| **Superior** | 5 points | +1/+1, 2 mod slots, 1-2 traits |
| **Masterwork** | 8 points | +1/+1, 2 mod slots, 2 traits, perfect craft |
| **Legendary** | 12 points | +2/+2, 3 mod slots, 2-3 traits, unique ability |
| **Artifact** | 18+ points | +3/+3, 4 mod slots, 3-4 traits, multiple abilities |

**Trait Cost Examples:**
- Basic traits (Keen, Quick Draw, Lightweight): 1 point
- Strong traits (Brutal, Rending, Igniting): 2 points
- Situational traits (Dragonbane, Sanctified, Silvered): 1 point
- Unique abilities: 3-5 points depending on power

---

## Design Guidelines for Unique Items

When creating Legendary/Artifact weapons and armor:

1. **Signature Ability** - One unique mechanic (teleport, AOE burst, summon, transform)
2. **Scaling** - Gets stronger at levels 5, 7, 9, or 10
3. **Mod Slots** - Always has 3+ slots so players can customize
4. **Name** - Evocative name that suggests its power
5. **Lore** - Brief backstory (who forged it, what it's killed, who wielded it)
6. **Visual Description** - What makes it look special/unique

**Bad Legendary:** "Longsword +2" with boring stat bonuses  
**Good Legendary:** "Dawnbreaker" - sword that blinds undead, calls down sunlight, and grows stronger at dawn

---

## "Rule of Cool" Override

When designing unique items: **Cool > Balanced**

If a Legendary weapon feels awesome to use, players will want it even if it's technically "weaker" in spreadsheet math. The Blink Strike teleport dagger might not outdamage a Superior dagger with perfect mods in sustained DPS, but the *fantasy* of being a teleporting assassin is worth more than +2 damage per turn.

## Crafting & Modification

### Weapon/Armor Modding
- Requires appropriate Temper unlocks (Blacksmithing, Leatherworking)
- Can swap mods during short/long rest
- Mods can be crafted or purchased
- Cost: 10-100 gold per mod depending on power

### Upgrading Quality
- Requires Master Smith unlock (7 Craft, Blacksmithing)
- Common → Fine: 100 gold, 1 day
- Fine → Superior: 500 gold, 1 week, rare materials
- Superior → Masterwork: 2000 gold, 1 month, exceptional materials

### Enchanting Basics
- Requires Minor Enchanting (7 Craft)
- Temporary enchantments last 24 hours
- Can add +1d4 elemental damage, +1 AR, or basic magic effects
- Cost: 50 gold per enchantment
