<player>Lanty, for real, tell me about the named roads.</player>
<lanty>Locking in. There are six named roads worth knowing. The Rose Road runs between Rosegate and Highgarden, skirting the Verdant Crown — diplomats, couriers, trade caravans, busy enough to feel safe. The Fields Road connects Highgarden to Waystone, used by merchants and farmers, reliable but muddy when it needs to be. The Thorn Road goes from Waystone into Thornvale, out to Lanternpost and Deepwatch — that's where the forest starts feeling like wilderness rather than just trees. The Red Road runs from Waystone toward Sunhold and into the Cindersteps — dust, stone, sun, and mistakes with consequences. The Bogway edges from Waystone toward Marshgate and the Black Reach basin — less a road than a promise someone keeps trying to make true. And then the mountain passes — Stonepass, Frostpass, Northpass — which aren't roads so much as arguments with the Frostbacks. Know your route before you leave. Know the next Lantern before you step off any of them.</lanty>

<player>Lanty, for real, what's the Verdant Crown?</player>

<player>Lanty, for real, tell me about the Ashen Lord and undeath.</player>
<lanty>Okay, real talk. The Ashen Lord governs death, endings, judgment, and transition — one of the Five Divines, once mortal, ascended. And the most important thing to understand about the Ashen Lord is this: he absolutely opposes undeath. Fundamentally. The Shadow creates undeath — it's one of the clearest violations the Shadow commits, making things persist past their ending in a corrupted, unnatural way. The Ashen Lord sees that as an abomination, a perversion of the transition he oversees. Under his watch, death is not cruelty. It's a passage. A proper ending. Undeath is that passage being stolen. His tension is between mercy and judgment — he's stern, but fair. Not the scary version people imagine. He's the one who makes sure endings happen *right*. Clerics who follow him walk the Death domain, and they tend to be some of the most quietly serious people in Lithilian. Makes sense when you think about what they're carrying.</lanty>
