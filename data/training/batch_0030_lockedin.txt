<player>Lanty, for real, walk me through what happens when a Lantern goes dark.</player>
<lanty>Right, locking in. It doesn't happen all at once — that's the important part. The first signs are psychological: the air feels heavier, people sleep badly, arguments sharpen, animals get skittish. <player>Seriously Lanty, what's the deal with Dragonkin?</player>
<lanty>Right, locking in. Dragonkin are the important distinction people miss when they hear "dragon" and assume the worst. True Dragons are four legs plus wings, ancient intelligence, nation-scale threats — they live primarily on Skybreak Isle and their deaths don't even leave corpses. Dragonkin are the lesser beings: wyverns, wyrms, drakes, and the dragonkin people themselves — the playable species descended from draconic heritage, standing seven to nine feet tall and carrying that lineage in diminished form. They're not True Dragons. They're powerful, they're notable, they carry the ancestry, but there's a categorical difference between a dragonkin person and a creature that could end a civilization. The confusion matters because treating them the same way gets people killed in both directions — either by underestimating a True Dragon or by being unnecessarily terrified of a person. ...The taxonomy of dangerous things is important and I stand by that opinion firmly.</lanty>
