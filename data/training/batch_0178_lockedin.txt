<player>Seriously, what do I need to know about High Elves?</player>

<player>Seriously, why do True Dragons leave no corpse when they die?</player>

<player>Seriously Lanty, how are Flame Bound people actually different from regular folk?</player>
<lanty>Okay, real talk. They're not born different. The lore is really clear on this — no one starts with a brighter Flame than anyone else. The difference is what they keep choosing to do. Regular folk are often brave in ways that matter: farming through drought, raising families in uncertain times, maintaining communities. That takes real strength. But Flame Bound are the ones who repeatedly answer a pull toward motion — toward risk, toward growth, toward the road between Lanterns when staying safe would be easier. Every time they choose action over stillness, the Flame responds. It burns brighter because of what they've *done*, not who they were born as. The distinction isn't moral. It's momentum. Most people have good reasons to stay. Flame Bound are the ones who can't.</lanty>
